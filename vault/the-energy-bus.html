The Fred Factor: Turning Transactions Into Interactions

5 min read · Turn transactions into interactions.
Start here

This one is dedicated to anyone who's ever been quietly amazed by someone doing an ordinary job in an extraordinary way — and wondered how to build a whole crew like that.

01The Friction

The tyranny of "just the job"

Most work can be done at a baseline of "technically fine." Fine is safe, forgettable, and everywhere. Whole crews run on autopilot, processing people instead of connecting with them.

The cost isn't a bad review. It's a floor with no pride in it — a place where nothing memorable ever happens because nobody decided it should.

02The Book on the Table

Meet Fred

Mark Sanborn's The Fred Factor is about a real mail carrier named Fred who turned an ordinary route into something remarkable through care, initiative, and a hundred small personal touches nobody required of him.

Fred proves the point: extraordinary isn't a job title or a budget. It's a decision available to anyone, in any role, on any shift.

03The Anchor

Turn every transaction into an interaction

The anchor is the heart of the whole brand: a transaction is an exchange, an interaction is a connection. Fred didn't deliver mail differently — he delivered himself along with it.

Every touchpoint on your floor is a fork: process the person, or connect with them. Extraordinary is just choosing connection more often than the job strictly requires.

04The Reframe

From doing the job to signing the work

A transactional crew asks "is this done?" A Fred crew asks "is this mine — would I put my name on it?" That pride is the difference between labor and craft.

You can't order it. You cultivate it — and it starts with how the manager treats the crew. People who feel like more than a transaction to you will treat guests like more than a transaction, without a single script.

05The Execution

Building a floor full of Freds

  • Catch and celebrate one "Fred moment" per shift — extraordinary spreads when it's seen and named.
  • Personalize one interaction on purpose today: a name, a memory, a genuine question.
  • Give the crew room to add their own touch; you can't mandate initiative, but you can make space for it.
  • Model it yourself first — be a Fred to your crew before you ask them to be Freds to the guest.
  • Ask each person: what's one small thing you could make remarkable this week? Then get out of the way.
06The Charge

Sign your work

Ordinary is always available. So is extraordinary — it costs the same amount of time and a completely different amount of heart.

Be a Fred. Build a crew of them.
